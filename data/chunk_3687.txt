Together with Ron they had spent most of the day seeking out those
people who had signed their names to the list in the Hog’s Head and
telling them where to meet that evening. Somewhat to Harry’s
disappointment, it was Ginny who managed to find Cho Chang and her
friend first; however, by the end of dinner he was confident that the
news had been passed to every one of the twenty-five people who had
turned up in the Hog’s Head. At half-past seven Harry, Ron, and
Hermione left the Gryffindor common room, Harry clutching a certain
piece of aged parchment in his hand. Fifth years were allowed to be
out in the corridors until nine o’clock, but all three of them kept
looking around nervously as they made their way up to the seventh
floor. “Hold it,” said Harry warningly, unfolding the piece of
parchment at the top of the last staircase, tapping it with his wand,
and muttering, “I solemnly swear that I am up to no good.” A map of
