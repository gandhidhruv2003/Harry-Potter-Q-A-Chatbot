The house-elf bowed low and addressed his own gnarled toes. “Master
said he wanted regular reports on what the Malfoy boy is doing, so
Kreacher has come to give —” Crack. Dobby appeared alongside Kreacher,
his tea-cozy hat askew. “Dobby has been helping too, Harry Potter!” he
squeaked, casting Kreacher a resentful look. “And Kreacher ought to
tell Dobby when he is coming to see Harry Potter so they can make
their reports together!” “What is this?” asked Hermione, still looking
shocked by these sudden appearances. “What’s going on, Harry?” Harry
hesitated before answering, because he had not told Hermione about
setting Kreacher and Dobby to tail Malfoy; house-elves were always
such a touchy subject with her. “Well . . . they’ve been following
Malfoy for me,” he said. “Night and day,” croaked Kreacher. “Dobby has
not slept for a week, Harry Potter!” said Dobby proudly, swaying where
he stood. Hermione looked indignant.
