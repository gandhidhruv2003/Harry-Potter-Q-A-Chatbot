Percy hissed, but Bagman didn’t seem to think the wand was rubbish at

seen one that convincing in years! I’d pay five Galleons for that!”
Percy froze in an attitude of stunned disapproval. “Boys,” said Mr.
Weasley under his breath, “I don’t want you betting. . . . That’s all
your savings. . . . Your mother —” “Don’t be a spoilsport, Arthur!”
boomed Ludo Bagman, rattling his pockets excitedly. “They’re old
