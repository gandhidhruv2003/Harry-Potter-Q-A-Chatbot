Through the dungeon wall burst a dozen ghost horses, each ridden by a
headless horseman. The assembly clapped wildly; Harry started to clap,
too, but stopped quickly at the sight of Nick’s face. The horses
galloped into the middle of the dance floor and halted, rearing and
plunging. At the front of the pack was a large ghost who held his
bearded head under his arm, from which position he was blowing the
horn. The ghost leapt down, lifted his head high in the air so he
could see over the crowd (everyone laughed), and strode over to Nearly
Headless Nick, squashing his head back onto his neck. “Nick!” he
roared. “How are you? Head still hanging in there?” He gave a hearty
guffaw and clapped Nearly Headless Nick on the shoulder. “Welcome,
Patrick,” said Nick stiffly. “Live ’uns!” said Sir Patrick, spotting
