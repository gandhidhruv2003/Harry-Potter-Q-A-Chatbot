The snake jerked its head toward Uncle Vernon and Dudley, then raised
its eyes to the ceiling. It gave Harry a look that said quite plainly:
“I get that all the time.” “I know,” Harry murmured through the glass,
though he wasn’t sure the snake could hear him. “It must be really
annoying.” The snake nodded vigorously. “Where do you come from,
anyway?” Harry asked. The snake jabbed its tail at a little sign next
to the glass. Harry peered at it. Boa Constrictor, Brazil. “Was it
nice there?” The boa constrictor jabbed its tail at the sign again and
Harry read on: This specimen was bred in the zoo. “Oh, I see — so
you’ve never been to Brazil?” As the snake shook its head, a deafening
shout behind Harry made both of them jump. “DUDLEY! MR. DURSLEY! COME
