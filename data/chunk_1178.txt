It was a sea of pointed black hats; each of the long House tables was
lined with students, their faces glimmering by the light of thousands
of candles, which were floating over the tables in midair. Professor
Flitwick, who was a tiny little wizard with a shock of white hair, was
carrying an ancient hat and a four-legged stool out of the hall. “Oh,”
said Hermione softly, “we’ve missed the Sorting!” New students at
Hogwarts were sorted into Houses by trying on the Sorting Hat, which
shouted out the House they were best suited to (Gryffindor, Ravenclaw,
Hufflepuff, or Slytherin). Professor McGonagall strode off toward her
empty seat at the staff table, and Harry and Hermione set off in the
other direction, as quietly as possible, toward the Gryffindor table.
