OTHER MINISTER t was nearing midnight and the Prime Minister was
sitting alone in his office, reading a long memo that was slipping
through his brain without leaving the slightest trace of meaning
behind. He was waiting for a call from the President of a far distant
country, and between wondering when the wretched man would telephone,
and trying to suppress unpleasant memories of what had been a very
long, tiring, and difficult week, there was not much space in his head
for anything else. The more he attempted to focus on the print on the
page before him, the more clearly the Prime Minister could see the
gloating face of one of his political opponents
