conjuring tight cords around Harry, tying him from neck to ankles to
the headstone. Harry could hear shallow, fast breathing from the
depths of the hood; he struggled, and the man hit him — hit him with a
hand that had a finger missing. And Harry realized who was under the
hood. It was Wormtail. “You!” he gasped. But Wormtail, who had
finished conjuring the ropes, did not reply; he was busy checking the
tightness of the cords, his fingers trembling uncontrollably, fumbling
over the knots. Once sure that Harry was bound so tightly to the
headstone that he couldn’t move an inch, Wormtail drew a length of
some black material from the inside of his cloak and stuffed it
roughly into Harry’s mouth; then, without a word, he turned from Harry
and hurried away. Harry couldn’t make a sound, nor could he see where
