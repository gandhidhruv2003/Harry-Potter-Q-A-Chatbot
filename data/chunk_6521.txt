“What are we doing now, Harry?” he asked. “You’ll see. Come on.”
Harry, Ron, and Hermione followed Bill up the steep stairs onto a
small landing. Three doors led off it. “In here,” said Bill, opening
the door into his and Fleur’s room. It too had a view of the sea, now
flecked with gold in the sunrise. Harry moved to the window, turned
his back on the spectacular view, and waited, his arms folded, his
scar prickling. Hermione took the chair beside the dressing table; Ron
sat on the arm. Bill reappeared, carrying the little goblin, whom he
set down carefully upon the bed. Griphook grunted thanks, and Bill
left, closing the door upon them all. “I’m sorry to take you out of
bed,” said Harry. “How are your legs?” “Painful,” replied the goblin.
