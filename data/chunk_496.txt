should run through the schedule one more time,” said Uncle Vernon. “We
should all be in position at eight o’clock. Petunia, you will be — ?”
“In the lounge,” said Aunt Petunia promptly, “waiting to welcome them
graciously to our home.” “Good, good. And Dudley?” “I’ll be waiting to
open the door.” Dudley put on a foul, simpering smile. “May I take
your coats, Mr. and Mrs. Mason?” “They’ll love him!” cried Aunt
Petunia rapturously. “Excellent, Dudley,” said Uncle Vernon. Then he
rounded on Harry. “And you?” “I’ll be in my bedroom, making no noise
and pretending I’m not there,” said Harry tonelessly. “Exactly,” said
Uncle Vernon nastily. “I will lead them into the lounge, introduce
you, Petunia, and pour them drinks. At eight-fifteen —” “I’ll announce
dinner,” said Aunt Petunia. “And, Dudley, you’ll say —” “May I take
you through to the dining room, Mrs. Mason?” said Dudley, offering his
fat arm to an invisible woman.
