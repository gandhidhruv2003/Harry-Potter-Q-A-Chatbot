holding a wand and poking happily at a slug in the grass, which was
swelling slowly to the size of a salami. As they drew level with him,
his mother came hurrying out of the tent. “How many times, Kevin? You
don’t — touch — Daddy’s — wand — yecchh!” She had trodden on the giant
slug, which burst. Her scolding carried after them on the still air,
mingling with the little boy’s yells — “You bust slug! You bust slug!”
