And to recover what we took . . .” Harry swam faster and soon saw a
large rock emerge out of the muddy water ahead. It had paintings of
merpeople on it; they were carrying spears and chasing what looked
like the giant squid. Harry swam on past the rock, following the
mersong. “. . . your time’s half gone, so tarry not Lest what you seek
stays here to rot. . . .” A cluster of crude stone dwellings stained
with algae loomed suddenly out of the gloom on all sides. Here and
there at the dark windows, Harry saw faces . . . faces that bore no
resemblance at all to the painting of the mermaid in the prefects’
bathroom. . . . The merpeople had grayish skin and long, wild, dark
green hair. Their eyes were yellow, as were their broken teeth, and
they wore thick ropes of pebbles around their necks
