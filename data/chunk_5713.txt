There was a rustle somewhere to their right: Yaxley drew his wand
again, pointing it over his companion’s head, but the source of the
noise proved to be nothing more than a pure-white peacock, strutting
majestically along the top of the hedge. “He always did himself well,
Lucius. Peacocks . . .” Yaxley thrust his wand back under his cloak
with a snort. A handsome manor house grew out of the darkness at the
end of the straight drive, lights glinting in the diamond-paned
downstairs windows. Somewhere in the dark garden beyond the hedge a
fountain was playing. Gravel crackled beneath their feet as Snape and
Yaxley sped toward the front door, which swung inward at their
approach, though nobody had visibly opened it. The hallway was large,
