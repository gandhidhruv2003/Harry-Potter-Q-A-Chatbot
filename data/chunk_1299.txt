“I see! Well, in that case, Potter, you’ll understand why I don’t
think it’s a good idea for you to be practicing Quidditch in the
evenings. Out on the field with only your team members, it’s very
exposed, Potter —” “We’ve got our first match on Saturday!” said
Harry, outraged. “I’ve got to train, Professor!” Professor McGonagall
considered him intently. Harry knew she was deeply interested in the
Gryffindor team’s prospects; it had been she, after all, who’d
suggested him as Seeker in the first place. He waited, holding his
breath. “Hmm . . .” Professor McGonagall stood up and stared out of
the window at the Quidditch field, just visible through the rain.
“Well . . . goodness knows, I’d like to see us win the Cup at last . .
. but all the same, Potter . . . I’d be happier if a teacher were
present. I’ll ask Madam Hooch to oversee your training sessions.” The
weather worsened steadily as the first Quidditch match drew nearer.
Undaunted, the Gryffindor team was training harder than ever
