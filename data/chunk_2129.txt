“Thanks, Hedwig,” he said, stroking her. She hooted sleepily, dipped
her beak briefly into his goblet of orange juice, then took off again,
clearly desperate for a good long sleep in the Owlery. There was a
pleasant feeling of anticipation in the air that day. Nobody was very
attentive in lessons, being much more interested in the arrival that
evening of the people from Beauxbatons and Durmstrang; even Potions
was more bearable than usual, as it was half an hour shorter. When the
bell rang early, Harry, Ron, and Hermione hurried up to Gryffindor
Tower, deposited their bags and books as they had been instructed,
pulled on their cloaks, and rushed back downstairs into the entrance
hall. The Heads of Houses were ordering their students into lines.
