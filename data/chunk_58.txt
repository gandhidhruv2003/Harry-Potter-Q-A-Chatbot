“Who’d be writing to you?” sneered Uncle Vernon, shaking the letter
open with one hand and glancing at it. His face went from red to green
faster than a set of traffic lights. And it didn’t stop there. Within
seconds it was the grayish white of old porridge. “P-P-Petunia!” he
gasped. Dudley tried to grab the letter to read it, but Uncle Vernon
held it high out of his reach. Aunt Petunia took it curiously and read
the first line. For a moment it looked as though she might faint. She
clutched her throat and made a choking noise. “Vernon! Oh my goodness
— Vernon!” They stared at each other, seeming to have forgotten that
