package and didn’t answer Aunt Petunia when she asked what he’d
bought. “Found the perfect place!” he said. “Come on! Everyone out!”
It was very cold outside the car. Uncle Vernon was pointing at what
looked like a large rock way out at sea. Perched on top of the rock
was the most miserable little shack you could imagine. One thing was
certain, there was no television in there. “Storm forecast for
tonight!” said Uncle Vernon gleefully, clapping his hands together.
“And this gentleman’s kindly agreed to lend us his boat!” A toothless
old man came ambling up to them, pointing, with a rather wicked grin,
at an old rowboat bobbing in the iron-gray water below them. “I’ve
already got us some rations,” said Uncle Vernon, “so all aboard!” It
was freezing in the boat. Icy sea spray and rain crept down their
