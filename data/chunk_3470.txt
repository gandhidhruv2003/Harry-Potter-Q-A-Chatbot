Friday dawned sullen and sodden as the rest of the week. Though Harry
glanced toward the staff table automatically when he entered the Great
Hall, it was without real hope of seeing Hagrid and he turned his mind
immediately to his more pressing problems, such as the mountainous
pile of homework he had to do and the prospect of yet another
detention with Umbridge. Two things sustained Harry that day. One was
the thought that it was almost the weekend; the other was that,
dreadful though his final detention with Umbridge was sure to be, he
had a distant view of the Quidditch pitch from her window and might,
with luck, be able to see something of Ron’s tryout. These were rather
feeble rays of light, it was true, but Harry was grateful for anything
