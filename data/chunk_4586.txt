Harry crossed to the door as fast as he could and pulled it open. He
was out in the sunshine again before Hagrid had finished saying good-
bye and walked away across the lawn. Once again, people called out to
him as he passed. He closed his eyes for a few moments, wishing they
would all vanish, that he could open his eyes and find himself alone
in the grounds. . . . A few days ago, before his exams had finished
and he had seen the vision Voldemort had planted in his mind, he would
have given almost anything for the Wizarding world to know that he had
been telling the truth, for them to believe that Voldemort was back
and know that he was neither a liar nor mad. Now, however . . . He
walked a short way around the lake, sat down on its bank, sheltered
from the gaze of passersby behind a tangle of shrubs, and stared out
over the gleaming water, thinking. .
